Theseus and The Abduction

When Theseus was coming of age he was set out to find a wife for himself. He set his eyes upon Helen of Tory. Although very young at the time, Theaseus saw Helen as very beautiful and was set to marry her when she is of age. Instead of waiting for the opportunity Theasus decided to go to Sparta, when Helen was around the age of 9 to 11, and abduct her. Theseus then brought her to his mother, in a small city outside of Athens, he asked his mother to raise her up until she is of age to marry. This was short lived however, shortly after her abduction Helen's brothers Castor and Pollux rescued Helen while, according to the myth, Theseus was sent to the underworld. Helen was then returned to Sparta where she lived the rest of her childhood.

Deiphobus the Opportunist Brother

During the War of Troy Paris's brother Deiphobus was fighting by his side Deiphobus was alot braver then his brother and apeared more heroic to the side of Troy and due to Paris's inexperience on the battlefeild Paris was killed in combat. The council of Troy was ready to end the war and was willing to give Helen back to Menelaus to end the fighting. But the beauty of Helen stole the heart of yet another man, Deiphobus, who ended up marrying Helen shorty after the death of his brother. The council didn't like this and they no longer saw Deiphobus as the hero he once was. Deiphobus was wed to Helen right up to the fall of Troy, where Deiphobus was killed and Helen was retuned to Menlaus, ending the 10 year war.
